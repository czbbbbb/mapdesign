# Quake Multiplayer Map Design
Development and notes designing my first quake map for level design

## Texturing 
#### _1. Immersion and Theme_
The map as of the v2.0 version is using default .wad(s) from Quake Remastered, the texture I chose for this map is mainly metal, some wood, and LED light blocks to evoke the theme of a lab that is still in construction process.

![imagealt](https://github.com/czbbbbb/mapdesign/blob/ec29a12934a5e5dcb95197b10360685adfd0339a/science1.png)

This images is the PC area of the map, the texture of screens evokes the theme of science and technology.

### _2. Guidance and Orientation_
I tried to make the map dimly lit, however it became a problem since some texture will not fully show up and will be really dark, I put a lot of lighting around the map plus the ceiling texture of a huge LED blocks to evoke the theme of a lab, where majority of space will be covered with lighting. 
![imagealt](https://github.com/czbbbbb/mapdesign/blob/5669ed7b0fa8ab6086bd17294f8116a03446ac53/science2.png)

The lighting comes from the tube to show that it's a lab, and small "light bulbs" around the edges of the objects to show that it is still in construction process and need light bulbs to lit up the area like street lamps. 

## Lighting 
### _1. Immersion and Theme_
The lighting of the map plays the role of upping visibility when playing more than trying to reach immersion since visibility in version 1.0 was horrible and players cannot see pick ups or obstacles like tables or crates to jump over.

![imagealt](https://github.com/czbbbbb/mapdesign/blob/ec29a12934a5e5dcb95197b10360685adfd0339a/science3.png)

The tube is the main focus of this area, I added the lighting around the area as a support for the lighting texture from the tube, since it is not enough to make players see that there's a section behind the tube.

### _2. Guidance and Orientation_
The lighting are put right above or slightly above weapon pickups and paths to highlight and show the player where to go (like a street lamp)

![imagealt](https://github.com/czbbbbb/mapdesign/blob/ec29a12934a5e5dcb95197b10360685adfd0339a/science4.png)

Putting lighting above or under something will act as a highlight and point players to focus on it.
